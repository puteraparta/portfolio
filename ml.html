Travel Insurance Claim Prediction Classification

Data Scientist Program - 2024
Github

A travel insurance company offers a variety of products tailored to travelers, including annual coverage, trip cancellation, and comprehensive insurance plans. These products are distributed through both agencies and online platforms, catering to a broad customer base. The Manager of Risk and Claims in the Insurance Department plays a key role in this initiative, overseeing risk evaluation, managing insurance claims, and designing strategies to minimize claim-related losses. The company aims to predict how likely policyholders are to file a claim, ensuring that resources are efficiently allocated for smooth claim processing without excess funds tied up in unused coverage.

The data analysis will explore patterns distinguishing policyholders who file claims from those who do not, while also evaluating claim potential across different insurance product types. To achieve this, a classification model will be developed using a variety of algorithms, including Logistic Regression, Decision Tree, KNN, Random Forest, XGBoost, and LightGBM. These algorithms will help forecast the likelihood of a policyholder filing a claim, providing actionable insights for the company.

Given the imbalanced nature of the data, the model will focus on both classes equally, with ROC/AUC serving as the primary evaluation metric. Balanced accuracy will be used instead of overall accuracy to account for the class imbalance. Once the top-performing model is identified, it will undergo hyperparameter tuning to further refine its performance and improve the company's ability to manage claims more effectively.
